4. Make a claim

You will need

Before you apply, make sure you have:

  • your National Insurance number
  • your doctor or health worker’s name and contact details
  • details of any time you’ve spent abroad
  • details if you're in hospital, a hospice, residential or nursing care

You will have to explain the details of your condition and how this affects you. You may be asked to send further evidence about your condition when making your application.

If you’re terminally ill you can get PIP more quickly if you’re not expected to live more than 6 months. You should have a DS1500 form from your doctor or health worker - this means you won't need a face-to-face assessment.
