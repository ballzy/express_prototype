Step 6 of 17

Your day-to-day activities

  • Do you need any help to prepare a simple meal for one from fresh ingredients?

    This includes food preparation such as peeling, chopping or opening a can, and safely cooking or heating food on a cooker hob or in a microwave oven.

  • Do you need any help to eat and drink?

    This means remembering when to eat, cutting food into pieces, putting food and drink in the mouth, and chewing and swallowing food and drink.

  • Do you need to monitor changes in your health condition, take medication or manage any treatments carried out at home?

    Monitoring changes includes monitoring blood sugar level, changes in mental state and pain levels. Home treatments include things like physiotherapy and home dialysis, regardless of whether these are NHS or private.

  • Do you need any help washing and bathing to keep your body clean?

    This means things like washing your body, limbs, face, underarms and hair, and using a normal bath or shower.

  • Do you need any help to go to the toilet or to manage incontinence?

    Go to the toilet means being able to get on and/or of a normal toilet, and cleaning yourself after using the toilet. Manage incontinence means emptying your bowel and bladder, including if you need a collecting device such as a bottle, bucket or catheter.

  • Do you need any help to dress or undress yourself?

    This means putting on and taking of appropriate clothes, including shoes and socks, knowing when to put on or take of clothes, and being able to select clothes that are appropriate.

  • Do you need any help to communicate with others?

    This means speaking to people in your native vocal language, and hearing and understanding what people are saying to you in your native vocal language.

  • Do you need any help to read and understand signs, symbols and words?

    This means signs, symbols and words written or printed in your native written language and doesn't include Braile.

  • Do you have any difficulties mixing with other people?

    This means how well you are able to get on with other people, understand how they’re behaving towards you and behave appropriately to them. It includes both people you know well and people you don’t know.
